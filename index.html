About.

I'm a biochemist and metabolic engineer who builds bridges between the wet lab and computation.

At Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, I lead a program on rare earth element biorecovery using Methylobacterium extorquens AM1 and drive high-throughput bioprocess development on Hamilton Vantage liquid handlers. Before LBNL, I earned a PhD in Chemistry from Texas A&M where I characterized metalloenzymes in the lumichrome catabolic pathway and studied C–S bond-forming cytochrome P450s.

I'm also pursuing an M.S. in Computer Science (Machine Learning) at Georgia Tech — deepening my work in predictive modeling, cheminformatics, and graph neural networks for substrate utilization prediction.
